When the plates collide with each other, it causes

(A) ** Faulting

(B) Folding

(C) Transform boundaries

(D) Earthquakes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As the plates rub against each other, huge stresses can cause portions of the rock to break, resulting in earthquakes. Places where these breaks occur are called faults.

Concept note-2: -Earthquakes occur along fault lines, cracks in Earthâ€™s crust where tectonic plates meet. They occur where plates are subducting, spreading, slipping, or colliding. As the plates grind together, they get stuck and pressure builds up.

Concept note-3: -A fault is a fracture or zone of fractures between two blocks of rock. During an earthquake, the rock on one side of the fault suddenly slips with relative to the other. The faults are most commonly found around the edge of plates which are continental-size blocks of rocks that comprise the outermost part of the earth.
